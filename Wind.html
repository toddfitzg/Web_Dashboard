Summary - Latitude and Wind


In this grapgh we are examining the effects latitude has on wind

takend from over 2000 different city locations around the world.



Based on the table that is presented the wind does not seem to be dependent upon the latitude


In order to understand how wind is effected in different parts of the world you need to take into account many more observations than just the latitude.


Wind is affected by tempurature. An easy hypothisis is the closer to the equator you get the warmer the weather should be and thus more wind.


Wind is caused by different pressure systems. It is the movement of air from a high pressure system to a low pressure system.


In order to have different pressure systems you need many things. One item is different terrain. Land surface typically heats up faster than water. Thus during the day you will often have off land wind. And at night when the land cools faster than the sea you have sea breezes.
